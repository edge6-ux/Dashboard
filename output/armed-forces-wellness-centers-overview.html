Armed Forces Wellness Centers — Overview

Generated by Gordon · March 22, 2026

What They Are

Armed Forces Wellness Centers are health and wellness facilities affiliated with the military that focus on preventive care, health promotion, and non-acute services for uniformed service members and eligible beneficiaries. They are designed to support readiness, resilience, and overall well-being through education, screening, and low-intensity clinical services.

How They Work

These centers typically offer services such as health risk assessments, fitness and nutrition counseling, tobacco cessation programs, stress management and behavioral health resources, vaccine and screening clinics, and referrals to higher-level care when needed. They may operate on a walk-in or appointment basis and often coordinate with larger Military Treatment Facilities (MTFs) to ensure continuity of care.

Ownership and Governance

Armed Forces Wellness Centers are generally government-run facilities, operated by the Department of Defense or by individual service branches (Army, Navy, Air Force, Space Force). In some cases, specific programs or support functions are delivered through contracted partners or local providers under government oversight, but overall governance and funding are governmental. They are not typically private or non-profit community clinics, though partnerships with non-profits or community organizations can occur for specific programs.

Who Can Use Them

Primary users include active-duty service members, and depending on the center, eligible Reserve/National Guard members, dependents, retirees, and other authorized beneficiaries. Access rules vary by location and the policies of the managing military health system.

Why They Exist

The primary goals are to maintain force readiness, prevent illness and injury, promote healthy behaviors, and reduce long-term healthcare costs by catching issues early and supporting healthier lifestyles.

Notes

Terminology and exact services vary by branch and location. Some installations refer to similar programs as "wellness centers," "health promotion clinics," or include wellness services within larger MTFs.
